Key terms on this page

Below are some terms used on this page that are used often. Knowing these defintions may allow you to understand this page more.

SUD: Stands for substance use disorder. This is a behavioral condition where someone uses a substance (legal or illegal) uncontrollably.

Illicit drugs: Drugs that are highly addictive and illegal.

Prevalence of use within (time period): The percentage of the population that used a particular substance within that time period.

Inhalants: As its name implies, inhalants are taken by sniffing or whiffing the substance to induce a euphoric effect. Examples may include nitrous oxide (also known as "balloons") or sniffing correction fluid, gasoline, felt-tip markers, glue, or spray paints.

Prescription drugs: Drugs that are controllably used for medical treatments, but can be misused. An example is opioids, which are used to help a person relieve pain. However, an opioid use disorder can result from usage.

Lifetime prevalence of use in the past year of selected substances among 12th graders, 2020

*Prevalence of use: The proportion of the population who has used a particular substance in a certain period. For example, if alcohol's prevalence of use within the past year is 40%, it means 40% of people used alcohol at least once in the past year.
